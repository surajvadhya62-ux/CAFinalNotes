Analyse whether GST applicable and who shall pay it? 1. Mentha arvensis sold by Veda Oils Pvt. Ltd.

2. Confiscated goods sold by Indian Railways to Karani Suppliers LLP (Registered in Delhi). 
Indian Railways - FCM applicable 
3. Priority Sector Lending Certificate sold by ICICI Bank (Registered) to HDFC Bank (Registered).  
HDFC Bank. RCM applicable. 
4. Transport of goods service by Nandan GTA to a factory in Pune (registered under Factories Act, 1948; but unregistered in GST).  
Factory shall pay tax under RCM. It shall have to take mandatory registration [sec. 24] for payment of tax. 
5. GTA services provided to an establishment of Delhi Gov., which has taken registration under GST only for TDS purpose, and not for making any taxable supply. 
Exempt. Entry 21B. 
6. SV (unregistered individual) supplied goods to AK (registered individual). SV is supposed to pay the freight of GTA which has been arranged for delivery of goods. GST on GTA service? 
GTA service is exempt, since recipient i.e. SV is an unregistered individual i.e. not covered in “specified recipient” 
7. Services w.r.t business development provided by Mr. Ganu Singhvi (Senior Advocate) to Adept Solutions Ltd (whose last year turnover was ₹ 68 Lacs) 
GST applicable. FCM applicable. As this is not a “legal service” case. 
8. Cloak room services by Indian Railways under various corporate bookings. Companies were liable to pay the consideration. 
Taxable. Entry 9E is applicable when such service is provided to Individuals. 
9. MH Gov. gave a commercial complex on rent to SV Traders Ltd- which has been incorporated in the current year only and is currently unregistered under GST. Rental value is ₹ 4,000 per month. 
Taxable. Exemption benefit due to “recipient being a small business entity” is NA to service of “renting of immovable property”. 
And since the Recipient is unregistered: FCM applicable 
 
10. What if in the above case, the rent was ₹ 4,000 for the entire year? 
Exempt. We shall be using the “value” based exemption i.e. ₹ 5,000 for entire year since it’s a case of continuous supply of services. 
11. A residential flat given on rent to Bombay Stationers Ltd. (registered under composition scheme) for stay of its MD Mr. Kishore Singa. 
Taxable. Recipient being registered under composition scheme is irrelevant here. That’s relevant in commercial dwelling RCM analysis. Supplier status irrelevant.

15. Shop given on rent by Mr. Kanwar Lal (Registered) to LG Electronics.
