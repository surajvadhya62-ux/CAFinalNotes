Going Concern and Non-Going Concern Valuation
• Valuation of business entity is made on assumption that it is going concern and will continue for
foreseeable future. Thus, valuation based on non-going concern assumption should be applied only
when investors are of the view that firm is not a going concern.
Going concern value is known as total value because ongoing firm will continue to earn profit which
contributes to its value in addition to its liquation value.
Non-going concern valuation is known as liquidation valuation because it is the net value realised
after disposing off all assets and discharging all liabilities.
